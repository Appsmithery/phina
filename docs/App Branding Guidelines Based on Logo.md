# Brand Guidelines

To create a cohesive experience for **phína**, the UI should feel like a high-end tasting room: airy, understated, and warm. Based on the logo we’ve refined, here are the branding guidelines to align your app’s interface.

## ---

**🎨 Color Palette**

The colors focus on "Natural Textures." Avoid stark blacks or pure whites to maintain that soft, sophisticated vibe.

| Element | Color Name | Hex Code | Usage |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Primary** | Vintage Rosé | \#B58271 | Buttons, active icons, and the logo itself. |
| **Secondary** | Cork Dust | \#D9BBAE | Secondary buttons, borders, and accents. |
| **Background** | Unbleached Paper | \#F2EFE9 | Main app background (easy on the eyes). |
| **Text** | Aged Oak | \#4A3B35 | Primary headings and body text. |

## ---

**🖋️ Typography**

The goal is a balance between "Heritage" (Serif) and "Modernity" (Sans-Serif).

### **Primary Heading: Playfair Display (Serif)**

* **Style:** Bold or Semi-Bold.
* **Usage:** Title headers, wine names, and "phína" branding.
* **Why:** It mirrors the elegant, high-contrast strokes of your logo's typeface.

### **Body & UI: Montserrat or Lato (Sans-Serif)**

* **Style:** Regular or Light.
* **Usage:** Descriptions, navigation labels, and buttons.
* **Why:** These are highly legible on mobile screens and keep the app feeling "app-like" and modern.

## ---

**✨ UI Design Principles**

* **Soft Corners:** Use a corner radius of **12px–16px** for cards and buttons. Avoid sharp 90-degree angles to keep the "soft" aesthetic.
* **Generous Whitespace:** Don’t crowd the vintage. Let the text breathe to mimic a minimalist wine label.
* **Micro-Animations:**
  * When a user clicks "Join" or "Uncork," use a subtle **upward-sliding animation** to mimic the "popped cork" motion from your logo.
* **Haptic Feedback:** Use a "light tap" haptic when selecting a wine, giving the tactile sensation of a glass clinking or a cork being pulled.

## ---

**📸 Imagery Style**

* **Lighting:** Warm, natural golden hour light.
* **Focus:** Shallow depth of field (blurred backgrounds) to highlight the texture of the wine and glass.
* **Content:** Focus on the "social" aspect—hands holding glasses, wine pouring, and close-ups of cork textures.
